Kick off mnemonics

How do you know when your team is ready to start a complex piece of work? You probably can’t ever know for sure, but perhaps there’s something you can do to reduce the risk of things going wrong once you start. 

How about a simple mnemonic, to help you remember to cover all the bases? Enter “BITMAP”. The power of a mnemonic, rather than just a list of ideas, is that you easily talk about it as a single thing. “Have you done the BITMAP?” , people might ask. Or “We need to have a BITMAP meeting.”#

So what does it mean? This is just an example that works nicely for a team I’m working with at the moment. It goes like this —
